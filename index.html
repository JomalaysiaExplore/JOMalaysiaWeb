What's
JOMalaysia Explore?

'JOMalaysia Explore' is a campaign project that helps people learn more about Malaysia by showcasing its diverse culture, including festivals, traditions, friendly folks, and everyday life. The goal of the campaign is to promote understanding between different cultures and to appreciate the uniqueness of Malaysia. It's all about making friends and getting to know Malaysia's culture better.

Malaysia is a multi-cultural society

The main ethnic groups are the native Malays as well as large populations of Chinese, and Indians. When visiting the country it is clear that the ethnicities retain their religions, customs and way of life. The most important festivals of each group are public holidays.

Muslim

A Malaysia Malay, often referred to as a "Malay," is an ethnic group native to Malaysia. They make up the majority of the country's population

Buddhist

Around 20% of the Malaysian population consists of Malaysia Buddhists of Chinese

Christian

The majority of Christians in Malaysia are in the western part of the country in regions like Sabah and Sarawak in East Malaysia

Indian

Indians in Malaysia are make up about 6% of the country's population. They have their unique culture, languages like Tamil and religions such as Hinduism
